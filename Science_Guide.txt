================================================================================
PHYSICS FUNDAMENTALS
================================================================================

CLASSICAL MECHANICS
- Newton's Laws:
  * First Law: Object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by force
  * Second Law: F = ma (Force equals mass times acceleration)
  * Third Law: For every action, there is equal and opposite reaction
- Kinematics: Study of motion
  * Velocity: rate of change of displacement (v = Δx/Δt)
  * Acceleration: rate of change of velocity (a = Δv/Δt)
  * Equations of motion: v = u + at, s = ut + ½at², v² = u² + 2as
- Dynamics: Study of forces causing motion
  * Friction: opposes motion (f = μN)
  * Normal force: perpendicular to surface
  * Applied force: external force on object

ENERGY AND WORK
- Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv² (energy of motion)
- Potential Energy: PE = mgh (gravitational potential energy)
- Elastic PE: PE = ½kx² (energy stored in spring)
- Work: W = Fd cosθ (force × distance × angle)
- Power: P = W/t (work per unit time)
- Conservation of Energy: Total energy remains constant in isolated system
- Efficiency: η = (useful output / total input) × 100%

MOMENTUM AND COLLISIONS
- Momentum: p = mv (mass × velocity)
- Impulse: J = FΔt = Δp
- Conservation of Momentum: Initial momentum = Final momentum
- Elastic Collision: kinetic energy is conserved
- Inelastic Collision: kinetic energy is not conserved
- Center of Mass: point where total mass appears concentrated

WAVES AND VIBRATIONS
- Wave Properties:
  * Wavelength (λ): distance between consecutive crests
  * Frequency (f): number of cycles per second (Hz)
  * Period (T): time for one complete cycle (T = 1/f)
  * Amplitude: maximum displacement from equilibrium
  * Speed: v = fλ
- Types of Waves:
  * Transverse: oscillation perpendicular to direction (light, strings)
  * Longitudinal: oscillation parallel to direction (sound, springs)
- Doppler Effect: apparent change in frequency when source moves
  * Moving toward observer: frequency increases
  * Moving away: frequency decreases
- Interference: superposition of waves
  * Constructive: waves add (amplitude increases)
  * Destructive: waves cancel (amplitude decreases)
- Resonance: maximum amplitude when driving frequency equals natural frequency

LIGHT AND OPTICS
- Properties of Light:
  * Visible spectrum: 400-700 nanometers
  * Speed in vacuum: c = 3×10⁸ m/s
  * Refractive index: n = c/v
- Reflection: light bounces off surface
  * Law of reflection: incident angle = reflected angle
  * Specular reflection: smooth surfaces
  * Diffuse reflection: rough surfaces
- Refraction: light bends when entering different medium
  * Snell's Law: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂
  * Critical angle: total internal reflection occurs
- Lenses:
  * Converging lens (convex): focuses light to focal point
  * Diverging lens (concave): spreads light apart
  * Focal length: f, lens equation: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v
- Optical Instruments: magnifying glass, microscope, telescope

ELECTRICITY AND MAGNETISM
- Electric Charge:
  * Charge (Q): fundamental property (Coulombs)
  * Coulomb's Law: F = kQ₁Q₂/r² (force between charges)
  * Electric field: region where charge experiences force
- Current and Voltage:
  * Current (I): flow of charge (Amperes) I = Q/t
  * Voltage (V): electric potential difference (Volts)
  * Ohm's Law: V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
- Resistance:
  * Resistivity (ρ): property of material
  * R = ρL/A (length/cross-sectional area)
  * Temperature affects resistance
- Power and Energy:
  * Electrical power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R (Watts)
  * Energy: E = Pt (Joules)
- Circuits:
  * Series: same current, voltages add, resistances add
  * Parallel: same voltage, currents add, 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂
  * Kirchhoff's Laws: current conservation, voltage conservation
- Magnetism:
  * Magnetic field: region where magnetic force acts
  * Magnetic flux: Φ = BA cosθ
  * Force on current-carrying wire: F = BIL sinθ
  * Lorentz force: F = q(v × B) for moving charge
- Electromagnetic Induction:
  * Faraday's Law: induced EMF = -dΦ/dt
  * Lenz's Law: induced current opposes change in flux
  * Transformer equation: V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂

================================================================================
CHEMISTRY ESSENTIALS
================================================================================

ATOMIC STRUCTURE
- Subatomic Particles:
  * Protons: positive charge, mass ≈ 1 amu, in nucleus
  * Neutrons: no charge, mass ≈ 1 amu, in nucleus
  * Electrons: negative charge, mass ≈ 1/1836 amu, in orbitals
- Atomic Number (Z): number of protons (identifies element)
- Mass Number (A): protons + neutrons
- Isotopes: same element, different number of neutrons
- Electron Orbitals:
  * s, p, d, f orbitals with different shapes
  * Aufbau principle: fill lowest energy first
  * Hund's rule: fill orbitals singly first with parallel spins
  * Pauli exclusion: maximum 2 electrons per orbital

PERIODIC TABLE
- Organization:
  * Periods: horizontal rows (electron shells)
  * Groups: vertical columns (similar properties)
- Element Properties:
  * Alkali metals (Group 1): very reactive, soft
  * Alkaline earth metals (Group 2): reactive, divalent
  * Halogens (Group 17): very reactive, form -1 ions
  * Noble gases (Group 18): unreactive, full valence shell
- Trends:
  * Atomic radius: increases down group, decreases across period
  * Ionization energy: increases across period, decreases down group
  * Electronegativity: increases across period, decreases down group
  * Metallic character: increases down group, decreases across period

CHEMICAL BONDING
- Ionic Bonds:
  * Transfer of electrons from metal to nonmetal
  * Forms cations (positive) and anions (negative)
  * High melting points, soluble in polar solvents
  * Electrostatic attraction
- Covalent Bonds:
  * Sharing of electron pairs
  * Nonmetals bonded to nonmetals
  * Single (1 pair), double (2 pairs), triple (3 pairs)
  * Polar: unequal sharing (different electronegativities)
  * Nonpolar: equal sharing (same electronegativities)
- Metallic Bonds:
  * Delocalized electrons in "electron sea"
  * Good conductors, malleable, ductile
  * High melting points
- Intermolecular Forces (van der Waals):
  * Dipole-dipole: between polar molecules
  * London dispersion: weak forces between all molecules
  * Hydrogen bonding: special dipole-dipole with H, N, O, F

MOLECULAR GEOMETRY
- VSEPR Theory (Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion):
  * Electron pairs repel and position to minimize repulsion
  * Steric number: bonding pairs + lone pairs
  * Linear (180°), Trigonal planar (120°), Tetrahedral (109.5°)
  * Trigonal bipyramidal (120°/90°), Octahedral (90°)
- Molecular Polarity:
  * Dipole moment: measure of polarity
  * Polar molecule: asymmetrical charge distribution
  * Nonpolar molecule: symmetrical or no dipoles

STOICHIOMETRY
- Atomic and Molar Mass:
  * Molar mass: mass of 1 mole in grams
  * Avogadro's number: 6.022 × 10²³ particles/mole
- Chemical Equations:
  * Balance atoms on each side
  * Coefficients represent molar ratios
  * Limiting reagent: reactant that runs out first
  * Theoretical yield: calculated maximum product
  * Percent yield: (actual/theoretical) × 100%
- Gas Laws:
  * Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT (P=pressure, V=volume, n=moles, T=temperature)
  * Combined Gas Law: (P₁V₁/T₁) = (P₂V₂/T₂)
  * Dalton's Law: P_total = P₁ + P₂ + ... (partial pressures)

CHEMICAL REACTIONS
- Types:
  * Combustion: reacts with oxygen (exothermic)
  * Combination: two substances form one compound
  * Decomposition: one compound breaks into simpler substances
  * Single displacement: element replaces another element
  * Double displacement: ions exchange between compounds
  * Acid-base: H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O
  * Redox: transfer of electrons
- Redox Reactions:
  * Oxidation: loss of electrons (oxidation state increases)
  * Reduction: gain of electrons (oxidation state decreases)
  * Oxidizing agent: causes oxidation (gets reduced)
  * Reducing agent: causes reduction (gets oxidized)

STATES OF MATTER
- Solid:
  * Fixed shape and volume
  * Particles tightly packed, vibrate in place
  * Crystalline (ordered) or amorphous (random)
- Liquid:
  * Fixed volume, takes shape of container
  * Particles close together, can move
  * Surface tension, viscosity properties
- Gas:
  * No fixed shape or volume
  * Particles far apart, move randomly
  * High kinetic energy
- Plasma:
  * Ionized gas with free electrons and ions
  * Found in stars, lightning, neon signs
- Phase Changes:
  * Melting: solid → liquid (absorbed heat)
  * Freezing: liquid → solid (released heat)
  * Vaporization: liquid → gas (absorbed heat)
  * Condensation: gas → liquid (released heat)
  * Sublimation: solid → gas (absorbed heat)

THERMOCHEMISTRY
- Enthalpy (H): total heat content of system
  * ΔH = H_products - H_reactants
  * Exothermic: ΔH < 0 (releases heat)
  * Endothermic: ΔH > 0 (absorbs heat)
- Entropy (S): measure of disorder/randomness
  * Increase in entropy favors spontaneous reactions
  * ΔS_surroundings > 0 for spontaneous at high T
- Gibbs Free Energy (G):
  * ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
  * ΔG < 0: spontaneous (forward reaction favored)
  * ΔG > 0: non-spontaneous (reverse reaction favored)
  * ΔG = 0: at equilibrium

CHEMICAL EQUILIBRIUM
- Equilibrium Constant (K):
  * K = [products]/[reactants] at equilibrium
  * K >> 1: reaction goes to completion
  * K << 1: very little product forms
  * K ≈ 1: significant amounts of both
- Le Chatelier's Principle:
  * Increase concentration: shifts away from that substance
  * Decrease concentration: shifts toward that substance
  * Increase temperature: shifts toward endothermic direction
  * Increase pressure: shifts toward fewer moles of gas
  * Catalyst: increases rate but doesn't shift equilibrium

================================================================================
BIOLOGY CORE CONCEPTS
================================================================================

CELL STRUCTURE AND FUNCTION
- Prokaryotic Cells:
  * No nucleus (DNA in nucleoid region)
  * No membrane-bound organelles
  * Bacteria and archaea
  * Smaller (typically 1-10 μm)
  * Ribosomes: 70S
- Eukaryotic Cells:
  * Nucleus contains DNA
  * Membrane-bound organelles
  * Animals, plants, fungi, protists
  * Larger (typically 10-100 μm)
  * Ribosomes: 80S
- Cell Membrane:
  * Phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins
  * Selectively permeable
  * Controls what enters and exits cell
- Major Organelles:
  * Nucleus: contains genetic material, controls cell
  * Mitochondria: powerhouse, ATP production (cellular respiration)
  * Endoplasmic Reticulum: rough (protein synthesis), smooth (lipid synthesis)
  * Golgi Apparatus: modifies and packages proteins
  * Lysosomes: contain digestive enzymes, break down waste
  * Ribosomes: site of protein synthesis
  * Centrioles: involved in cell division
  * Cytoskeleton: provides cell structure and movement

CELL DIVISION
- Mitosis: produces 2 identical daughter cells (somatic cell division)
  * Prophase: chromosomes condense, spindle forms
  * Metaphase: chromosomes align at cell equator
  * Anaphase: sister chromatids separate
  * Telophase: nuclear envelopes reform
  * Cytokinesis: cytoplasm divides
- Meiosis: produces 4 haploid gametes (sex cells)
  * Meiosis I: homologous chromosomes separate (reduces ploidy)
  * Meiosis II: sister chromatids separate
  * Results in genetic variation through crossing over and independent assortment

CELLULAR RESPIRATION
- Glycolysis (in cytoplasm):
  * Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) → 2 Pyruvate
  * Net: 2 ATP + 2 NADH
  * Anaerobic process
- Citric Acid Cycle (in mitochondrial matrix):
  * Acetyl-CoA → CO₂
  * Produces: NADH, FADH₂, ATP, GTP
  * Aerobic process
- Electron Transport Chain (inner mitochondrial membrane):
  * NADH and FADH₂ donate electrons
  * Electrons move through proteins, creating proton gradient
  * ATP synthase: chemiosmosis produces ~30-32 ATP per glucose
- Fermentation (anaerobic):
  * Lactic acid fermentation: pyruvate → lactate (muscles during exercise)
  * Alcohol fermentation: pyruvate → ethanol + CO₂ (yeast, bacteria)

PHOTOSYNTHESIS
- Light Reactions (in thylakoid membranes):
  * Photosystem II: absorbs light, splits water (2H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻)
  * Electron transport chain: pumps H⁺ across membrane
  * Photosystem I: reduces NADP⁺ to NADPH
  * ATP synthase: creates ATP via chemiosmosis
  * Products: ATP, NADPH, O₂
- Calvin Cycle (in stroma):
  * Carbon fixation: CO₂ + RuBP → 3-PG (catalyzed by RuBisCO)
  * Reduction: 3-PG → G3P (uses ATP, NADPH)
  * Regeneration: G3P → RuBP (uses ATP)
  * Products: glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)
- Overall: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

GENETICS AND HEREDITY
- DNA Structure:
  * Double helix of nucleotides (A, T, G, C)
  * Sugar-phosphate backbone
  * Base pairs: A-T (2 bonds), G-C (3 bonds)
  * Antiparallel strands
- DNA Replication:
  * Semi-conservative: each strand serves as template
  * DNA polymerase: adds nucleotides
  * Leading strand: continuous synthesis 5'→3'
  * Lagging strand: discontinuous (Okazaki fragments)
- Transcription (DNA → RNA):
  * RNA polymerase: reads template strand
  * mRNA: carries genetic code (A, U, G, C)
  * tRNA: transfers amino acids
  * rRNA: part of ribosome
- Translation (mRNA → Protein):
  * Codons: 3 nucleotides code for amino acid
  * Start codon: AUG
  * Stop codons: UAA, UAG, UGA
  * 64 codons code for 20 amino acids
- Genes and Alleles:
  * Gene: segment of DNA coding for protein
  * Allele: different versions of gene
  * Dominant: expressed in heterozygotes
  * Recessive: only expressed in homozygotes
- Mendelian Inheritance:
  * Law of Segregation: alleles separate during gamete formation
  * Law of Independent Assortment: genes assort randomly
  * Punnett square: predicts offspring genotypes
- Mutations:
  * Point mutation: single nucleotide change
  * Insertion/Deletion: adds or removes nucleotides
  * Chromosome mutations: large-scale changes
  * Most are neutral, some beneficial, some harmful

EVOLUTION
- Evidence for Evolution:
  * Fossils: record of past life
  * Comparative anatomy: similar structures in different species
  * Molecular evidence: DNA/protein similarities
  * Biogeography: distribution of species worldwide
  * Observed evolution: bacteria resistance, peppered moths
- Natural Selection:
  * Variation: individuals differ in traits
  * Heredity: traits can be inherited
  * Differential reproduction: some survive/reproduce better
  * Result: allele frequencies change over time
- Speciation:
  * Reproductive isolation: populations can't interbreed
  * Geographic isolation: separated by barriers
  * Behavioral isolation: different mating behaviors
  * Temporal isolation: breed at different times
- Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium:
  * p² + 2pq + q² = 1 (genotype frequencies)
  * Predicts allele frequencies stay constant without evolution

ECOLOGY
- Population Dynamics:
  * Birth rate, death rate, immigration, emigration
  * Exponential growth: doubling under ideal conditions
  * Logistic growth: limited by carrying capacity
  * Population density affects survival
- Community Interactions:
  * Predation: predator eats prey
  * Competition: organisms compete for resources
  * Symbiosis: mutualism (+/+), parasitism (+/-), commensalism (+/0)
  * Herbivory: animals eat plants
- Energy Flow:
  * Producers (plants): fix solar energy via photosynthesis
  * Primary consumers (herbivores): eat plants
  * Secondary consumers (carnivores): eat herbivores
  * Tertiary consumers: eat secondary consumers
  * Decomposers: break down dead matter
  * Energy efficiency: ~10% transferred at each trophic level
- Biogeochemical Cycles:
  * Carbon cycle: CO₂ ↔ atmosphere, biosphere, lithosphere
  * Nitrogen cycle: atmosphere → soil → organisms → decomposers
  * Water cycle: evaporation, condensation, precipitation, infiltration
  * Phosphorus cycle: weathering → soil → organisms → decomposers

HUMAN BODY SYSTEMS
- Digestive System:
  * Mouth: mechanical and chemical digestion (amylase)
  * Stomach: acid digestion of protein (pepsin)
  * Small intestine: chemical digestion and nutrient absorption
  * Large intestine: water absorption, fecal formation
  * Pancreas: produces digestive enzymes
  * Liver: bile production aids fat digestion
- Circulatory System:
  * Heart: pumps blood
  * Arteries: carry blood away from heart
  * Veins: return blood to heart
  * Capillaries: site of gas and nutrient exchange
  * Blood: carries oxygen, nutrients, hormones, waste
- Respiratory System:
  * Nose/mouth: warm and filter air
  * Trachea/bronchi: conduct air to lungs
  * Lungs: gas exchange in alveoli
  * Diaphragm: muscle controlling breathing
  * Hemoglobin: carries oxygen in blood
- Nervous System:
  * Brain: central control center
  * Spinal cord: transmits signals
  * Neurons: transmit electrical/chemical signals
  * Synapses: gaps between neurons
  * Neurotransmitters: carry signals across synapse
- Endocrine System:
  * Pituitary: master gland, controls other glands
  * Thyroid: regulates metabolism
  * Adrenals: stress response (adrenaline, cortisol)
  * Pancreas: blood glucose regulation (insulin, glucagon)
- Immune System:
  * Innate immunity: non-specific (fever, inflammation, lysozyme)
  * Adaptive immunity: specific to pathogen (B cells produce antibodies, T cells)
  * Vaccination: primes immune system without causing disease

REPRODUCTION
- Asexual Reproduction:
  * Binary fission: bacteria divide into two identical cells
  * Budding: organism grows bud that detaches
  * Fragmentation: organism breaks into pieces
  * Advantages: rapid, no mate needed
  * Disadvantages: no genetic variation
- Sexual Reproduction:
  * Gamete formation: meiosis produces haploid sex cells
  * Fertilization: sperm + egg = diploid zygote
  * Advantages: genetic variation, adaptation
  * Disadvantages: slower, need to find mate
- Human Reproduction:
  * Male: testes produce sperm
  * Female: ovaries produce eggs
  * Menstrual cycle: ~28 days, ovulation ~day 14
  * Pregnancy: fertilization → implantation → development → birth

EARTH SCIENCE
================================================================================

PLATE TECTONICS
- Earth's Structure:
  * Crust: thin solid outer layer (oceanic, continental)
  * Mantle: hot rock, convection drives plate movement
  * Core: outer (liquid iron/nickel), inner (solid iron)
- Plate Tectonics Theory:
  * Lithosphere divided into plates
  * Plates move slowly (cm/year)
  * Continental drift: continents move over time
  * Evidence: fossil distribution, seafloor spreading, paleomagnetic
- Plate Boundaries:
  * Convergent: plates collide (subduction, mountain building, earthquakes)
  * Divergent: plates separate (ocean ridge, seafloor spreading, rifts)
  * Transform: plates slide past (earthquakes, no crust creation/destruction)
- Earthquakes:
  * Caused by sudden release of stress at faults
  * P-waves: fast, compress and stretch (arrive first)
  * S-waves: slower, perpendicular motion (arrive second)
  * Surface waves: slowest, most damaging
  * Magnitude: Richter scale (logarithmic, energy released)
  * Intensity: Modified Mercalli scale (observed damage)

ROCKS AND MINERALS
- Minerals:
  * Inorganic solids with orderly crystal structure
  * Defined chemical composition
  * Physical properties: hardness (Mohs scale), luster, streak, color
  * Examples: quartz, feldspar, mica, calcite
- Igneous Rocks (cooled/solidified magma):
  * Intrusive: cooled slowly underground (granite, diorite)
  * Extrusive: cooled quickly at surface (basalt, obsidian)
  * Mafic: dark, dense, Fe/Mg rich (basalt)
  * Felsic: light, less dense, Si/Al rich (granite)
- Sedimentary Rocks (compacted sediment):
  * Classified: sandstone, siltstone, shale (clastic)
  * Chemical: halite, limestone (precipitate from solution)
  * Bioclastic: chalk, coquina (organic)
  * Fossils commonly found
- Metamorphic Rocks (altered by heat/pressure):
  * Contact metamorphism: near igneous intrusion
  * Regional metamorphism: mountain building
  * Examples: schist, gneiss, marble, slate
  * Foliiation: banded appearance from directed pressure

WEATHERING AND EROSION
- Mechanical Weathering:
  * Frost wedging: water freezes in cracks
  * Exfoliation: surface sheets separate
  * Abrasion: grinding by sediment
- Chemical Weathering:
  * Oxidation: iron combines with oxygen
  * Hydrolysis: water breaks bonds
  * Dissolution: minerals dissolve in water
  * Acid rain: accelerates chemical weathering
- Erosion:
  * Gravity: direct downslope movement (landslides)
  * Water: streams, rivers carve valleys
  * Ice: glaciers transport massive amounts
  * Wind: deflation removes fine particles
  * Results: canyons, deltas, depositional features

ATMOSPHERE
- Composition:
  * Nitrogen (78%), Oxygen (21%), Argon (0.93%), CO₂ (0.04%), trace gases
- Layers:
  * Troposphere: weather occurs, temperature decreases with altitude
  * Stratosphere: ozone absorbs UV, temperature increases
  * Mesosphere: coldest layer
  * Thermosphere: temperature increases
  * Exosphere: outer edge, space
- Weather:
  * Air pressure: high pressure = fair weather, low = storms
  * Humidity: amount of water vapor
  * Temperature: affects weather patterns
  * Wind: caused by pressure differences
  * Clouds: water droplets suspended in air
- Climate:
  * Long-term weather patterns
  * Determined by latitude, ocean currents, elevation
  * Climate zones: tropical, subtropical, temperate, polar
- Greenhouse Effect:
  * CO₂, CH₄, H₂O trap heat
  * Natural: keeps Earth habitable
  * Enhanced: human activities increase greenhouse gases
  * Global warming: rising average temperatures

OCEANS
- Properties:
  * Salinity: ~35 ppt (mostly NaCl)
  * Temperature: varies with depth and latitude
  * Density: increases with depth
- Ocean Currents:
  * Surface currents: wind-driven (gyres)
  * Deep currents: density differences
  * Gulf Stream: warm water northward
  * Thermohaline circulation: global conveyor
- Tides:
  * Caused by Moon's gravity (Sun contributes)
  * Spring tide: Sun and Moon aligned (maximum range)
  * Neap tide: Sun and Moon perpendicular (minimum range)
  * Daily tides from Moon's rotation
- Marine Zones:
  * Epipelagic (photic): light, photosynthesis
  * Bathypelagic (abyssal): dark, low temperature, high pressure
  * Hadal: deepest trenches
  * Benthic: ocean floor

================================================================================
SCIENTIFIC METHOD REVIEW
================================================================================
1. Observation: gather information about natural world
2. Hypothesis: make testable prediction
3. Experiment: design controlled test of hypothesis
   - Independent variable: what you change
   - Dependent variable: what you measure
   - Control group: unchanged
   - Experimental group: with treatment
4. Analysis: examine results using statistics
5. Conclusion: interpret results relative to hypothesis
6. Report: communicate findings (peer review)

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

Physics:
Q1: What is the SI unit for force? Answer: Newton (N)

Biology:
Q7: What is the process by which plants make glucose? Answer: Photosynthesis
Q8: How many ATP are produced in aerobic respiration? Answer: ~30-32 ATP
Q9: Define natural selection. Answer: Differential survival and reproduction

Earth Science:
Q10: What causes earthquakes? Answer: Release of stress at faults
Q11: What are the three types of rocks? Answer: Igneous, sedimentary, metamorphic
Q12: What is the main gas in Earth's atmosphere? Answer: Nitrogen (78%)

LABORATORY SAFETY
================================================================================
- Always wear protective equipment: safety goggles, lab coat, gloves
- Know location of fire extinguishers, safety showers, eyewash stations
- Never eat, drink, or apply cosmetics in laboratory
- Wash hands before and after lab work
- Report accidents, spills, and injuries immediately
- Dispose of chemical waste in designated containers
- Read Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) for chemicals
- Don't pipette by mouth - use pipette bulbs
- Tie back long hair, wear closed-toe shoes
- Be aware of hot equipment and surfaces
- Keep work area clean and organized
- Follow all written procedures
